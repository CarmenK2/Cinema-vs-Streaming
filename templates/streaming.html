Cinema Ticket Price vs Streaming Price


Move your cursor to the circle labels to display info


According Screen Australia, the average cinema ticket price was $15.24 in 2021 while our data shows the average price for streaming services is $11.91. There are plenty of fees for streaming platforms to compete for the eyeball. This chart compares the streaming provider's fee and their number of simultanceous streams. In terms of pricing, cinema ticket price is definitely competitive and not the reason cinema-goers will be detered by. The restriction and health protocol does accelerate the adoption of signing up streaming services while we cannot physically go to cinema.

We asked the question - How do boxoffice movie releases compare to how often people attend the cinema per person per year and how does the number of subscribers to streaming services compare to that? The graph indicates a steady release of boxoffice movies until 2020 when Covid hit with between 800 and 1000 releases per year. Up until 2020 the frequency of how often people would go to the movies amounted to between 6-7 times per year. There was a sharp decline in 2020 due to Covid restictions and temporary shut downs due to social distancing measures. Streaming services entered the stage in 2017 since then the number of streaming service subscribers has exploded to over 30 million subscribers. In summary the number of box office released are relatively similar throughout 2016-2019 and so is the frequency of attendance per person per year whilst the streaming numbers are growing.
